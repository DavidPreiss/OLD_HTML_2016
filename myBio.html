My Bio

This web page is being written as a requirement for my ENGR0011 class but the content in the page is mine and what is posted is my choice and is not the position of the university and does not reflect on the university policies.

My name is David Preiss. I was born in Arlington Hospital on Thanksgiving of 1997. My parents are Robert and Valerie Preiss. I have no brothers or sisters and am an only child.

I attended Arlington Science Focus School from 2003 - 2009. I lived very close so I walked to school. I attended Swanson Middle School from 2009 - 2012. I lived pretty far from Swanson so I had to take the bus. I attended Washington-Lee High School 2012-2016. Washington-Lee was in between Swanson and ASFS so I walked, but it was a bit of a hike. I am now enrolled in the Swanson School of Engineering at the University of Pittsburgh. I plan on majoring in Computer Engineering and then becoming a computer engineer.

My favorite food is gumbo. There was once a time when I thought that the idea of a favorite food was silly. How can you have a favorite? It all depends on your mood right? One day you might crave a juicy steak, the other, a hot pumpkin pie. This was all before I had gumbo.

My favorite movie is Flatland. The kiddy one not the wierd one. This is simply because by being about 2-dimensional beings trying to comprehend the 3rd dimension, it creates a fantastic platform for understanding the 4th dimension from the perspective of 3-dimensional beings. It is also presented in such a way that even a child could understand it.

I really enjoy flipping balisong knives, otherwise known as butterfly knives. After getting my hands on one, I quickly started carrying it everywhere, compulsively doing the tricks that I was rapidly teaching myself. As you can imagine, this was a bit of a problem for a child attending high school. I stopped because it was also becoming legitimately addicting. To this day I sometimes consider getting back into it, only to decide not to out of the fear that I'd never get out.
